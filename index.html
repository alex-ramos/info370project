Does the legalization of marijuana affect crime rates in Denver, CO?

Final project for Info 370

Abstract

With voting season wrapping up, what better topics to examine than those currently on 5 states ballots, like the topic of recreational marijuana. While many states like Colorado and Washington have already legalized the recreational use of marijuana, there is some ambiguity on the effects legalization has had besides raising tax revenue. Voters who support legalization believe that the drug is not dangerous and that the amount of money the marijuana industry makes can help the economy of the states who have legalized it. However, opponents of legalization claim that more marijuana in the city would raise the rate of crime. Colorado, one of the first states to have legalized the recreational use of marijuana, has developed a lot of public data about crime and dispensaries, and we used their datasets for this research.

Using the marijuana related crime data provided by The State of Colorado and the location of recreational marijuana stores we could conduct an analysis on the proximity of stores in relation to crime. In addition to the trends in specific crimes we would also include the changes in crime overall.
